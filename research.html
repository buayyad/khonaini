Research

Exploring politics, governance, and civic engagement in Kuwait and the Gulf region.

PhD Research

Abdullah's primary research examines the genealogy of parliamentary committees' formation and the political and power dynamics of the Kuwait National Assembly (KNA). This work is conducted as part of the Al-Sabah Programme at Durham University's School of Government and International Affairs, where he holds the General Sir Peter de la Billière Doctoral Fellowship.

His research adopts a comparative politics framework, analyzing the institutional evolution and power dynamics within Kuwait's parliamentary system. The study seeks to understand how committee structures shape legislative outcomes and reflect broader political negotiations within Kuwaiti society.

Research Areas

Parliamentary Politics

Genealogy of parliamentary committees' formation and the political and power dynamics of the Kuwait National Assembly. Institutional analysis of legislative processes and committee structures.

Youth & Citizenship

Youth citizenship and civic engagement. Participatory action research in educational contexts, exploring how young people understand and exercise their political agency.

Urban Governance

Urban governance and segregation in Kuwait. Investigating how spatial politics and urban planning intersect with citizenship, identity, and social stratification.

Democratic Participation

Freedom of expression, constitutional rights, transparency, and the mechanisms through which citizens engage with democratic institutions in the Gulf region.
